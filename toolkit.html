NotebookLM

NotebookLM is an AI-powered research assistant designed for academic work. It grounds its responses exclusively in the sources you upload (such as PDFs, Docs, and websites), providing inline citations to minimise hallucination and support rigorous scholarship.

Key Academic Benefits

  • Grounded Responses: By citing source passages, NotebookLM minimises hallucinations, making it suitable for rigorous academic use.
  • Enhanced Guidance: Automatically generate study materials, timelines, and FAQs to support teaching, revision, or publication workflows.
  • Collaborative Work: The Plus edition supports co-editing and tracking contributions, which is ideal for research teams or tutorials.
  • Reproducible Outputs: Citations and transcripts maintain transparency for peer review and scholarly rigour.

Core Features

  • Summarise and interact with uploaded sources via chat Q&A.
  • Generate study guides, timelines, FAQs, and briefing documents.
  • Produce "Audio Overviews" — podcast-style summaries featuring AI hosts.

Google AI Studio

Google AI Studio is a web-based platform for experimenting with and building applications on top of Google's Gemini family of models. It provides an intuitive interface for academics to prototype ideas, generate code, and analyse multimodal data.

Core Functionalities

  • Advanced Text Analysis: Summarise papers, draft grant proposals, and generate educational content using various prompt styles.
  • Multimodal Capabilities: Process and analyse information from images, audio, and video for innovative research.
  • Code Generation: Describe a function in natural language to rapidly generate code in various programming languages, ideal for prototyping tools and automating pipelines.

Data Privacy: Free vs. Paid Tiers

Free Tier Warning

Google may use content from the free service to improve its AI models, and data may be reviewed by humans. Do not input sensitive or confidential research data into the free version.

Paid Tier Recommendation

For sensitive data, the paid tier (via Google Cloud Billing) is recommended. Under this plan, Google commits not to use your data for training its models, ensuring enhanced privacy.

Citation & Discovery Tools

Find papers faster, visualise literature, and keep references clean. Use these alongside your library databases.

Research Discovery

  • Connected Papers — map related work & seminal papers
  • Research Rabbit — explore author & topic networks
  • Elicit — AI-assisted paper finding & summarising
  • Consensus — search claims with evidence from papers

References & Citations

  • Zotero — free reference manager + web clipper
  • Mendeley — reference management & PDF annotation
  • Paperpile — simple reference management for Google Docs
  • Google Scholar — quick look-ups; verify metadata/DOIs
Tip: Always verify metadata (authors, year, DOI) against the PDF or publisher page before you cite.
