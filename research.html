Always Check Your Institution's AI Policy

Universities have different rules about AI use. Some allow it for brainstorming, others require declaration, and some prohibit it entirely for certain assignments. When in doubt, ask your lecturer.

Using AI for Student Research

üéØ Remember: AI is your research assistant, not your replacement. Use it to enhance your thinking, not to do your thinking for you.

AI can revolutionise how you approach research and writing, but it requires the right mindset. Think of AI as a sophisticated research assistant that can help you find sources, understand complex topics, organise your thoughts, and improve your writing - whilst you remain the author and critical thinker.

From brainstorming essay topics and finding relevant sources to getting feedback on your drafts and ensuring proper citations, AI tools can support every stage of your research process. The key is learning to use these tools ethically and effectively whilst developing your own academic skills.

This guide will show you practical ways to integrate AI into your research workflow whilst maintaining academic integrity and producing work that is genuinely your own.
